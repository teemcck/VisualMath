What is a Riemann Sum?

A Riemann sum is a method used to approximate the area under a curve over a given interval by dividing it into a finite number of rectangles. Each rectangle's area is calculated by multiplying the function's value at a specific point by the width of the interval. The accuracy of this approximation depends on the number of rectangles used and how they are defined. This simulation provides an interactive way to visualize the process of constructing Riemann sums and explore how different parameters affect their calculation.
